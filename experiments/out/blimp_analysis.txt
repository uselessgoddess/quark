  If true, the advantage must be specific to the long-distance case.
  BLiMP ships the same construction at both distances. Compare:

  paradigm                                loop12   dense   delta      z

  loop12's edge WITHOUT the long distance: +1.30 points

  The edge is there when the distance is not. It is a property of the

  what produced the number, so MoR does not follow from it.

=== 4. What 0.60% and 2.30% actually mean ===

  paradigm                              run         score   sigma vs chance
  wh_vs_that_with_gap_long_distance     loop12       2.30             -30.2
  wh_vs_that_with_gap_long_distance     dense        0.60             -31.2
  wh_vs_that_with_gap                   loop12       4.60             -28.7
  wh_vs_that_with_gap                   dense        3.30             -29.5

  Chance is 50%. Both models sit ~30 SD BELOW it: they do not lack
  the preference, they hold the inverted one, ~99% of the time. These
  minimal pairs differ by one function word (who/that), so the score is
  a readout of which word the corpus made more likely. A model that
  fits the corpus better follows that prior harder and scores LOWER.
  Dense fits better (word ppl 108.275 vs 115.163), and scores lower.
  'Winning' here is a symptom of being the weaker LM, not of skill.
